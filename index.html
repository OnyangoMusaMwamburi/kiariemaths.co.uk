About Me

I have a vivid memory from school of my maths teacher asking me if I would consider being a teacher to which my grimacing face could tell a thousand NOs. The irony is not only the creation of this website but my passion to teach mathematics and sharing its hidden beauty is genuine and pure.

Why genuine? I am sick and tired of the justification (especially from politicians) citing the utility of the subject or its significance in the modern economy. I personally believe this has had the unintended consequence of destroying the beauty and soul of the subject where the pedagogy of mathematics is akin to robots crunching algorithms, completely devoid of the human spirit. In Hardy’s ‘a mathematician apology’ the only justification mathematics need is the same for that of art or music. I have a BEng in Chemical Engineering (2011) from Birmingham University UK and an MSc in Mathematical Modelling and Scientific Computing from Oxford (2015). In addition, I have had the opportunity to work across various fields from finance, machine learning and of course my favourite which has been tutoring.

About this site

I created this site with secondary/high school children in mind and in particular those who are considering to major in mathematics, engineering or any scientific field. There is a general feeling that most of high school mathematics is pre-occupied with tricks, step by step calculations and fail to remind the students of the big picture of mathematics which is about ideas and concepts. Moreover, computers are ubiquitous and are a million times better than humans at calculations. A paradigm shift to focusing on how to conceptualise and solve problems mathematically while getting computers to do the calculating should be the new normal in the pedagogy of mathematics. The mathematician Conrad Wolfram addresses this issue better than I will ever do and has a dedicated website.

My primary goal is to address a profound aspect of mathematics that is hardly captured if ever by school mathematics, abstraction and its importance in problem solving. I have set out to achieve this by presenting some areas of abstract algebra such as group theory often encountered in the later years of undergraduate to early graduate mathematics. The material covered has been made accessible by doing away with arcane proofs and rigour.
